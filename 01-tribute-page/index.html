Dalton Trumbo

Renowned American screenwriter, novelist, and revolutionary.

A brief timeline of the life of Dalton Trumbo:

  • 1905 - Born in Montrose, Colorado.
  • 1924 - Begins studies at University of Colorado Boulder.
  • 1930 - Finishes studies at University of Southern California.
  • 1935 - Published his first novel, Eclipse.
  • 1936 - Joined the Screen Writers Guild.
  • 1939 - Won the National Book Award for Most Original Book for his novel Johnny Got His Gun.
  • 1940 - Nominated for an Academy Award for Best Adapted Screenplay for Kitty Foyle
  • 1943 - Joined the Communist Party in the United States.
  • 1947 - Summoned to testify before the House Un-American Activities Committee (HUAC) on the subject of his political affiliations.
  • 1950 - Served 10 months in federal prison on contempt of Congress charges. Moved to Mexico upon his release after being blacklisted from the film industry.
  • 1954 - Won the Academy Award for Best Story for Roman Holiday despite not being credited due to his ongoing blacklisted status.
  • 1960 - Received first film credit since blacklist began, for Exodus.
  • 1973 - Diagnosed with lung cancer.
  • 1975 - Received his first Academy Award which retroactively recognized his work on The Brave One (1956).
  • 1976 - Dalton Trumbo died in Los Angeles, California at the age of 70.
